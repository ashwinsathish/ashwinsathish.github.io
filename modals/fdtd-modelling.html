Overview

Comprehensive investigation of electromagnetic wave propagation through inhomogeneous concrete-rebar structures using Finite-Difference Time-Domain (FDTD) simulation. The primary goal was to quantify and analyze signal delay and wave characteristics across different corrosion levels, providing insights into material degradation and wave interaction mechanisms.

Simulation

The simulation employed a multi-dimensional FDTD approach, systematically analyzing electromagnetic wave propagation through:

  • 1D scenario: Linear concrete medium with rust section
  • 2D scenario: Planar concrete-rebar-rust cross-section
  • 3D scenario: Cuboidal rebar embedded in concrete block

System Model

Detailed electromagnetic model of concrete-rebar system with precise material and geometric characterization:

  • Block Dimensions: 10cm x 10cm x 10cm
  • Rebar Material: Steel with varying corrosion levels (0% to 30%)
  • Material Properties:
    • Concrete: Relative permittivity ϵ_r = 4 - j0.06
    • Rust: Relative permittivity ϵ_r = 10 - j0.86

Middle Z Slice View of Concrete-Rebar System (rs=6, rr=3)

Methodology

Computational techniques used to simulate electromagnetic wave propagation were:

  • Spatial and Temporal Interleaving for Numerical Stability
  • Mur's 2nd Order Absorbing Boundary Conditions
  • Sine-modulated Gaussian Pulse Excitation (7.5 GHz)
  • Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) Analysis

Results

Comprehensive analysis of electromagnetic wave propagation and signal characteristics across different corrosion levels:

  • 1D Simulation: Direct correlation between rust layer thickness and signal delay
  • 2D Simulation: Complex wave interactions and frequency domain characteristics
  • 3D Simulation: Exponential increase in signal delay with corrosion progression

Conclusions

The study demonstrated the effectiveness of FDTD algorithms in simulating electromagnetic wave propagation through inhomogeneous lossy media. Key findings include:

  • Quantitative relationship between corrosion levels and signal propagation
  • Detailed insights into wave interactions in multi-layered media
  • Potential applications in non-destructive testing and infrastructure monitoring
