Overview

This project presents a surface plasmon resonance (SPR) sensor based on a 1D photonic crystal design, utilizing aluminium as the plasmonic metal stacked with silicon and platinum diselenide layers. The implementation demonstrates high sensitivity and figure of merit values, making it a promising solution for biosensing applications.

Sensor Architecture

The sensor structure comprises an aluminum layer (30nm) over a CaF2 prism, followed by three stacks of silicon (40nm) and platinum diselenide (4nm), with a 2500nm thick analyte layer. Key design features include:

  • Aluminum: Selected for low cost, narrow linewidth, and maximum penetration depth at higher wavelengths
  • Silicon: Chosen as low RI material for straightforward fabrication and photonic integration
  • Platinum Diselenide: Utilized for high carrier mobility (3000 cm²V⁻¹s⁻¹), tunable bandgap, and chemical inertness

Performance Optimization

Layer optimization was performed through systematic analysis of component thicknesses and stack numbers:

  • Silicon layer thickness optimized to 40nm, balancing FWHM and minimum reflectivity
  • PtSe2 thickness of 4nm achieved highest sensitivity and figure of merit
  • Three Si-PtSe2 stacks provided optimal interaction length for guided modes

Key Results

The optimized sensor structure, operating at 1550nm wavelength, demonstrated exceptional performance metrics:

  • Sensitivity: 101.1°/RIU
  • Figure of Merit: 1531.82 RIU⁻¹
  • Effective operation in near-infrared spectrum, ideal for biological tissue analysis
