Overview

This project presents a high-resolution Aluminum-based plasmonic device incorporating metamaterials for cancer cell detection. The sensor uses a metal-dielectric-metal configuration with Al metal and Barium titanate, enhanced by a TiO2 coating and MoS2 binding layer, achieving sensitivity of 101.2°/RIU and figure of merit of 5060 RIU⁻¹.

Layer Optimization

Optimized layer thicknesses: BTO (5nm), Metamaterial (160nm), and TiO2 (2nm) provide maximum Figure of Merit and Quality Factor for the sensor structure.

The SPR curve for the optimized structure (Struc 8 in the figure above) demonstrates a sharp resonance dip and clear shift in resonance angle for different refractive indices, indicating high sensitivity and resolution.
