About Me

I recently graduated from BITS Pilani with a dual degree in Mathematics and Electronics & Instrumentation Engineering. I've always been drawn to the space where abstract mathematical models meet physical hardware — which led me to spend my final year in different research labs across Germany. My research has progressed through different layers of wireless technology. From building virtualized 5G testbeds in my bachelor's thesis to simulating reflecting surfaces during my master's — I've been able to indulge myself (atleast as a tiny speck) in frontier telecom tech. I now begin my PhD journey in Integrated Sensing and Communications (ISAC), focusing on situational awareness and digital twins. Excited to see how this field shapes up in the next few years!

Outside work, you'll likely find me on a badminton court or sitting down at the piano for a few hours (Garageband has been a recent obsession!)

Research Interests
